About
Bridging human & machine visual intelligence

I am a Cognitive Science PhD student at Johns Hopkins University, working with Prof. Leyla Isik. I am fascinated by how intelligent systems—biological and artificial—learn to see and understand the world. My research sits at the intersection of mechanistic interpretability, visual psychophysics, and multimodal AI.

My journey began with a BS-MS at IISER Bhopal in Electrical Engineering & Computer Science. Before JHU, I spent a year as Lab Manager at Brown's Serre Lab with Prof. Thomas Serre, and worked with Prof. Mirta Galesic at the Santa Fe Institute and Prof. Vineeth Balasubramanian at IIT Hyderabad.

A recurring thread: I am fascinated by what features and abstractions guide recognition and decision-making, and how we can design AI systems more aligned with human perception. I aspire to pursue an academic career and become a professor.

Research Directions
What I work on & why it matters
01
Visual Cue Analysis & Bias
Investigating how color, texture, shape, and spatial frequency interact as cues in vision models. Developed causal graph methods and rank-based metrics to quantify bias—revealing links between architecture and human perceptual strategies.
02
Mechanistic Interpretability
Localizing circuits and attribute-based activations in multimodal models like CLIP. Building frameworks to unlearn spurious concepts via dictionary-based pruning, making models more controllable and robust.
03
Bio-Plausible Vision Models
Developing foveation-inspired approaches for scale-invariant recognition in the HMAX framework. Conducting psychophysical experiments to align model and human preferences for spatial frequency bands.
04
Neural Dynamics Modelling
Applied linear state-space models to model hippocampal neural dynamics for non-spatial stimuli—a periodic structured framework presented at NeurIPS'24 WiML and under review at COSYNE'25.
05
Belief Dynamics in Networks
Developed a theoretical network-science framework to analyze the formation and spread of beliefs in online social networks, in collaboration with Prof. Mirta Galesic at the Santa Fe Institute.
06
Multi-Agent RL & Graph Networks
Built a dynamic GNN for centralized training with decentralized execution in persistent visual modeling—Bachelor's thesis at IISER Bhopal's Moon Lab under Dr. Sujit PB.
