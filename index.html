I am a PhD student in Computational Cognitive Science at Johns Hopkins University, advised by Prof. Leyla Isik. I study how biological and artificial visual systems build abstractions to recognize objects, understand scenes, and generalize across contexts.

A central question driving my work is how multimodal concepts are represented across different model architectures: how does language supervision reshape visual representations, and where do unimodal and multimodal systems converge or diverge in the features they prioritize? I develop causal and circuit-based frameworks to understand how low-level cues (color, texture, spatial frequency, shape) are composed into higher-level object representations, and how this compares to human perceptual strategies.

Outside of research, I play piano, make art, run, and read non-fiction. I also spent a formative year as chief editor of Chrysalis, IISER Bhopal's science communication magazine - an experience I hope to build on.
